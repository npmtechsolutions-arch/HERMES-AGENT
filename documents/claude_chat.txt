Yes — and good eye, because that's a genuinely different *category* from what you've been specifying, and worth being precise about so you build the right thing.

Wispr Flow is a **dictation utility**: a system-wide layer that turns speech into cleaned-up text wherever your cursor is — any app, any text field, with AI tidying up filler words and formatting. It's a horizontal input tool, not an assistant that *does tasks*. What you've built (HERMUS) is the opposite end: agents that take an instruction and go execute work. They're complementary, not the same thing.

Here's the important distinction for your roadmap — there are really **two voice paradigms**, and you currently have one:

- **Voice-to-action** (what HERMUS does): "follow up with the Mehta lead" → an agent performs a task. This is your core.
- **Voice-to-text** (what Wispr Flow does): you speak, polished text appears in whatever you're typing into — an email, a WhatsApp box, a code comment, a patient note. This is *dictation*, and you don't really have it as a standalone capability.

So yes, this can absolutely be included — and there's a strong strategic reason to: **dictation is the perfect top-of-funnel and daily-habit hook.** People open a task-doing assistant a few times a day; they use a dictation layer *hundreds* of times a day. It creates the daily active usage that subscription retention lives on, and it's a far easier first "wow" than autonomous agents (lower trust barrier — it just types what you said, nothing scary).

How it fits your specific strengths:
- **Voice-first** — you already have the STT pipeline; a system-wide dictation overlay is a focused extension of it, not a new stack.
- **Local-privacy** — this is your real edge here. Wispr Flow and most dictation tools process speech in the cloud. A **local, on-device dictation layer** ("your dictation never leaves your machine") is a genuine differentiator for exactly your buyers — doctors dictating patient notes, lawyers dictating drafts, anyone handling confidential content who *can't* send it to a cloud transcription service. That's a compliance-grade selling point competitors structurally can't match.
- **Per-profession vocabulary** — your phrase packs (medical terms, legal terms, "GSTR-2B", "OMR") make dictation *accurate* for each vertical, which generic tools struggle with.

Where it's hard (so you build with eyes open): a true system-wide overlay that injects text into *any* application is OS-level work — global hotkeys, accessibility APIs, focus tracking, per-app text injection on Windows/macOS/Linux. It's not trivial, and it's a different engineering muscle than your agent backend. The realistic sequence is: dictation *inside* HERMUS first (dictate into agent commands, notes, comms drafts — you nearly have this), then a system-wide overlay as a dedicated module once the core product has traction.

How I'd package it: not as a separate product, but as a **module** (call it HERMUS Dictate / Voice Type) that's bundled into every edition and role app, and also offered as a cheap standalone hook — a ₹499–999/month "local private dictation" entry product that competes directly with Wispr Flow on the privacy angle and pulls users into the ecosystem, where they later discover the agents.
