# HERMUS — Help & User Guide
### A friendly, complete guide to running your AI company
**Product:** HERMUS — your AI Office Assistant
**Audience:** Business owners and their staff (the people using the product day-to-day)
**Document version:** 1.0

---

## Welcome 👋

HERMUS is **a company, not a chatbot**. Instead of one assistant, you get a whole team of AI "employees" that answer your customers, follow up on leads, handle paperwork, and tell you what's happening — all governed by you, and (on the desktop app) running privately on your own machine.

This guide explains, in plain language, what each part does and how to use it. You don't need to be technical. If you can send a message, you can run HERMUS.

> **Tip:** Almost everything in HERMUS can be done by **talking or typing** to the floating microphone button in the bottom-right corner. If you're ever lost, click it and ask: *"What can you do?"*

## 1. Signing in

1. Open HERMUS. You'll see a sign-in card.
2. Make sure the **Account Owner** tab is selected (that's you, the business).
3. Enter your email and password and click **Sign in**.

**Demo accounts** (for trying things out):
- **Account Owner** — `user@gmail.com` / `user`
- **Product Admin** — `admin@gmail.com` / `admin` (this is for the people who run the HERMUS platform itself, not for business users)

To leave, click **Sign out** at the bottom of the left menu.

---

## 2. Finding your way around

The screen has three parts:

- **Left menu** — your modules, grouped into:
  - **Pipeline** — your leads and appointments (the labels change to match your industry).
  - **Workspace** — your company, AI team, tasks, automations.
  - **Intelligence** — your inbox, memory, and analytics.
  - **Trust & Resilience** — backups, safety, compliance.
  - **Account** — marketplace, subscription, devices, settings.
- **Top bar** — a search/ask box, a **Simple / Advanced** switch, the theme (light/dark) toggle, notifications, and your profile.
- **The microphone orb** (bottom-right) — your voice assistant, always one click away.

### Simple vs Advanced
Use the **Simple / Advanced** switch in the top bar:
- **Simple** — plain language, fewer numbers. Best for everyday use.
- **Advanced** — shows the technical detail (logs, rules, data) for power users.

## 3. Talking to HERMUS

Click the **microphone orb** (bottom-right). A panel opens. You can **speak** (push to talk) or **type**.

You can do two kinds of things:

**Ask about your business** — and get a real answer instantly:
- "How many leads did I get today?"
- "What's my no-show rate?"
- "How many hours did my agents save this week?"
- "What's my pipeline conversion?"

**Give a command:**
- "Show my tasks"
- "Hire an employee"
- "Give me my briefing"
- "Send me a daily summary at 8am"

If HERMUS doesn't understand, it will gently tell you what it *can* help with. There are also tappable example questions to get you started.

## 4. Your daily routine

**Home** is your dashboard. Each morning:

1. Open **Home**.
2. Read (or press **Play** to hear) your **Daily Briefing** — a 30-second summary of yesterday and today, plus anything that needs attention ("2 no-shows", "a backup is overdue", "3 approvals waiting").
3. Check **Approvals** (in the Workspace menu) — anything your AI wants your sign-off on appears here. Approve or decline.
4. Glance at the **live activity** feed to see what your AI team is doing right now.

That's it — a few minutes and you know exactly where your business stands.

## 5. Setting up your company

Open **Workspace → Your Company**. This is where HERMUS becomes *your* company:

1. Enter your company details and choose your **industry**.
2. HERMUS suggests the right **AI employees and workflows** for your industry (a mix of curated picks and AI suggestions).
3. Click **Apply** on a suggestion and HERMUS builds your starting team and processes for you.

**Faster option — Vertical Agents:** Open **Workspace → Vertical Agents** for ready-made, named products (Dentist Agent, Restaurant Agent, Real Estate Agent, and more). Click **Deploy** and you instantly get a full, working setup for that business — team, automations, a front-desk chatbot, and the right compliance — and your screens start speaking that industry's language.

**Even more focused — Solutions:** Open **Workspace → Solutions** for single-job helpers (Missed-Call Responder, Review Booster, No-show Prevention…). Deploy one in a click.

## 6. Your AI team

### Org Chart (your employees)
Open **Workspace → Org Chart** to see your AI workforce as an org chart. Click to **hire** a new AI employee — give it a name, a job title, a capability level, and the skills/tools it's allowed to use. You're the manager; they do the work.

### Agent Team (the team that talks to your customers)
Open **Workspace → Agent Team**. This is special: instead of one bot, you get a **team that routes and collaborates**.

- A **manager** agent reads each customer message and sends it to the right specialist (Reservations, Billing, Support…).
- Specialists **consult each other** when a question spans two areas — so a customer asking about *both* a booking *and* a bill gets one good answer.
- If an agent isn't sure, or the customer asks for a person, it **hands off to a human** (you) — never guessing.

The four tabs:
- **Test chat** — talk to your team like a customer and watch how it routes and collaborates.
- **Team & routing** — see your agents, what each handles, and the safety limits.
- **Human inbox** — where handoffs land. Open one, edit the suggested reply, and resolve it.
- **Adversarial** — try tricky messages (angry customers, trick questions) before you go live.

**To get started:** open Agent Team and click **Deploy demo team** to see it all working with sample data.

## 7. Answering customers

### Chatbots
Open **Workspace → Chatbots** to create individual assistants (a Sales Bot, an HR Bot…). For each one you set:
- a **name and personality**,
- which **knowledge** it can use,
- which **channels** it answers on (your website, WhatsApp, Telegram, and more).

Use the built-in **test chat** to try it. HERMUS answers using *your* information and shows where each answer came from — and if it doesn't know, it says so instead of making something up.

> **The golden rule:** HERMUS never invents facts to your customers. It answers from your knowledge or hands off — by design.

## 8. Leads & appointments

Your **Pipeline** menu (at the top of the left menu) is where customer interest turns into business. The labels match your industry — "Leads / Site Visits", "Patient Inquiries / Appointments", and so on.

**Leads / Inquiries:**
- Every new inquiry is captured, **scored** (hot/warm/cold), and followed up automatically on a sensible schedule.
- HERMUS won't message people who've opted out, and you can **undo** a message before it sends.
- You can **take over** any conversation from the AI at any time.
- Every helpful action quietly logs the **time it saved you**.

**Appointments / Visits:**
- Book a slot from a lead; HERMUS sends reminders (a day before, and a couple of hours before).
- Mark visits done or no-show, and jot the outcome (you can even do this by voice).

## 9. Getting work done

HERMUS gives you four levels of automation — pick whatever fits:

- **Recipes** (*easiest*) — open **Workspace → Recipes**, flip a switch ("Confirm tomorrow's appointments", "Ask happy customers for reviews", "Brief me every morning"), tune one setting, done.
- **Tasks** — open **Workspace → Tasks** and ask for something ("Prepare a welcome email"). HERMUS breaks it into steps and does it, keeping you posted.
- **Workflows** — open **Workspace → Workflows** and just *describe* a process in a sentence ("When a new lead comes in, qualify it and book a visit"). HERMUS turns your words into a working flow.
- **Pipelines** — open **Workspace → Pipelines** to chain several AI employees into a bigger process, with your approval at key steps and a report at the end.

**Rehearsal** (**Workspace → Rehearsal**) lets you safely *practise* a process with pretend customers — nothing real is ever sent — so you can build confidence before going live.

**Skills** (**Workspace → Skills**): as your agents work, they learn reusable skills. You review and approve them — they get better over time, but only with your say-so.

## 10. Knowledge & insights

- **Comms Hub** (**Intelligence → Comms Hub**) — one tidy inbox where messages are sorted into **Urgent / Action / FYI / Spam**, each with a ready-to-send draft reply.
- **Second Brain** (**Intelligence → Second Brain**) — your private memory. Save notes, policies, and documents; search them instantly. This is what your chatbots use to answer accurately. It all stays on your machine.
- **Knowledge Graph** (**Intelligence → Knowledge Graph**) — a visual map of the people, places, and things in your business and how they connect.
- **Analytics** (**Intelligence → Analytics**) — charts showing your activity, the hours your AI saved you, and trends.

## 11. Trust, safety & your data

HERMUS is built so you stay in control:

- **Backup & Restore** (**Trust & Resilience → Backup & Restore**) — your data lives on your machine, so it's yours to protect. Create an **encrypted backup** (keep the recovery phrase safe!) and restore any time. HERMUS reminds you if a backup gets stale.
- **Compliance & Isolation** — a **tamper-evident record** of important actions you can verify at any time, plus rules and limits that keep your AI inside safe boundaries.
- **Reliability** — quality checks that test your agents and catch problems before customers do.
- **Trust & Governance** — a plain-language health check and the "why" behind any AI decision.
- **Webhooks** — connect HERMUS to your other tools so they react to events automatically.
- **Remote Access** & **Model Gateway** — securely reach your system from elsewhere, and choose how powerful (and fast) the AI should be.

**Your privacy:** on the desktop app, the AI runs **locally** — your business data never leaves your computer.

## 12. Your account

- **Marketplace** (**Account → Marketplace**) — add industry templates, agent packs, skills, and integrations. Installing an industry pack re-themes HERMUS to suit you.
- **Subscription** (**Account → Subscription**) — your plan, what's included, your usage, and invoices.
- **Devices** (**Account → Devices**) — the computers connected to your account; remove one to free a slot.
- **Settings** (**Account → Settings**) — your profile, voice preferences, privacy controls, and accessibility options (including larger text).

## 13. Quick-start checklists

### First 10 minutes
1. Sign in as the Account Owner.
2. Open **Vertical Agents** and **Deploy** the one that matches your business (or set up via **Your Company**).
3. Open **Agent Team → Test chat** and ask it a customer-style question.
4. Open **Recipes** and switch on "Brief me every morning".
5. Open **Home** and press **Play** on your Daily Briefing.

### Every morning
1. **Home** → read/Play the Daily Briefing.
2. **Approvals** → clear anything waiting.
3. **Pipeline** → check new leads and today's appointments.

### Before going live with customers
1. **Agent Team → Adversarial** → try the tricky personas.
2. **Reliability** → run the quality checks.
3. **Backup & Restore** → take a fresh backup.

## 14. Frequently asked questions

**Is my data private?**
Yes. On the desktop app the AI runs on your own machine and your business data stays local.

**Do I need to know anything technical?**
No. Use **Simple** mode and the microphone orb. Advanced mode is there if you want the details.

**Will the AI ever make something up to a customer?**
No — by design. It answers from your saved knowledge or hands the conversation to you.

**What happens if the AI is unsure?**
It hands off to you. Handoffs appear in the **Agent Team → Human inbox** with a suggested reply you can edit and send.

**Can the AI message people who don't want to be contacted?**
No. Opt-outs are respected, and you can undo a message before it sends.

**How do I see how I'm doing?**
Ask the orb ("How many leads today?"), read your **Daily Briefing**, or open **Analytics**.

**Can I try things without affecting real customers?**
Yes — use **Rehearsal** (practise with pretend contacts) and the **Test chat** in Agent Team and Chatbots.

**Why is a reply sometimes slow?**
When the local AI is thinking (especially on a normal computer without a powerful graphics card), replies can take a little time. HERMUS shows it's working and never freezes.

**What's the difference between Chatbots and Agent Team?**
A **Chatbot** is one assistant. **Agent Team** is several specialists that route and collaborate, with built-in human handoff — best for handling real customer conversations.

**What do I do with the recovery phrase from a backup?**
Keep it somewhere safe. You need it to restore an encrypted backup. Without it, the backup can't be opened.

## 15. Troubleshooting

**I can't sign in.**
Check you're on the right tab (Account Owner vs Product Admin) and your email/password are correct. Try the demo login to confirm the app is reachable.

**The page is empty / no data.**
That's normal on a fresh start — HERMUS begins empty. Use a **Deploy** button (Vertical Agents, Solutions, or Agent Team's "Deploy demo team") to add sample data, or set up via **Your Company**.

**The AI's answers are short or generic.**
The local AI may be off or busy. Answers still work using built-in logic, just with less nuance. Check the **Model Gateway** screen to see available models.

**My screens are labelled for the wrong industry.**
Open **Universal Core** (or install the right pack in **Marketplace**) and re-skin to your industry.

**A reply seems to hang.**
Give it a moment — local AI can be slow on CPU-only machines. The app stays responsive; you can keep using other screens.

**I don't see my customer data in the admin account.**
That's intentional. The **Product Admin** account only sees aggregate platform data — never your business content. Use the **Account Owner** login for your business.

**Something needs my approval and I'm not sure why.**
Open **Approvals** for the details, or check **Trust & Governance** for the "why" behind the decision.

## 16. Glossary

- **Account Owner** — you, the business running HERMUS.
- **Product Admin** — the operator of the HERMUS platform itself (not a business user).
- **Agent / AI employee** — an AI worker with a name, a job, skills, and tools.
- **Agent Team (multi-agent)** — a manager agent plus specialists that route and consult each other to answer customers.
- **Manager / router agent** — the agent that decides which specialist should handle a message.
- **Consult** — when one specialist asks another for help within the same conversation.
- **Handoff** — passing a conversation from the AI to a human (you).
- **Grounded answer** — an answer based on your saved knowledge, with sources — not invented.
- **Guardrail** — a safety check (e.g. blocking manipulation attempts or off-limits topics).
- **Confidence** — how sure an agent is; low confidence triggers a handoff.
- **Pipeline** — a multi-step process; also the menu group for your leads and appointments.
- **Recipe** — a one-switch automation.
- **Workflow** — a process you describe in words that becomes an automated flow.
- **Second Brain** — your private, searchable memory.
- **Daily Briefing** — your spoken/typed morning summary.
- **Re-skin / industry skin** — re-labelling the product to match your industry.
- **Vertical Agent** — a ready-made, named product for one industry (e.g. Dentist Agent).
- **Solution** — a single-job ready-made agent (e.g. Missed-Call Responder).
- **Approval** — an action that waits for your sign-off.
- **Rehearsal** — safe practice mode; nothing real is sent.
- **Escalation** — a handoff item in the Human inbox.
- **Budget gate** — a limit that stops runaway AI usage/cost before it happens.
- **Tamper-evident audit** — a verifiable record of important actions that reveals any meddling.
- **Local-first** — the AI runs on your own machine; your data stays private.
